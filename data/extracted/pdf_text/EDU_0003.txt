(d) 	 Are all angles made by vertical lines with horizontal lines 
	
equal?
All the angles of the above square grid are equal. The angles 
∠XYA, ∠XYB, ∠POB, ∠POA, ∠RSB and ∠RSA formed by 
the lines XY, PO and RS on the line AB have equal measure, 
i.e. 90°. All angles formed by vertical lines with horizontal 
lines are 90° and so, they are all equal. Two straight lines that 
make 90° at the point of intersection to each other are called 
perpendicular lines.
 	
Two lines that make an angle of 90° to each other are 
called the perpendicular lines. In the above figure, XY, 
PO and RS are perpendicular with AB. It can be written 
as XY⊥ AB, PO ⊥ AB and RS ⊥AB.
In a square grid, all the vertical and horizontal lines are 
perpendicular to each other.

There are four squares from A to C. Similarly, there are four squares 
from B to D. The distance from A to C and from B to D are equal. In 
such condition, the lines AB and CD are parallel to each other. This 
is denoted by AB // CD.
If the distance between the two straight lines on the plane surface 
is always the same, then those lines are called parallel lines. 
Horizontal lines in the square grid are parallel to each other. 
Similarly, vertical lines in the square grid are parallel to each
