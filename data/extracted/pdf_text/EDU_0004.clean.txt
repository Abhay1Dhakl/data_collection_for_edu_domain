3.1 Simplifications for fundamental operations of a problem
Read, discuss and learn
Solution of problems using four basic rules of mathematics; addition (+), sub-
straction (-), multiplication (x) and division (/) is called simplification. During
simplification multiplication or division whichever comes first can be operated.
Then addition or substraction which ever comes first can be operated.
Example1:
Simplify : 15 ÷ 5 x 3 + 7 - 15
This problem when expressed in words respresents; 5 parts of 15 is
multiplied by 3 which is added with 7 and 15 is substracted from it becomes
easier to simplify with order of division, multiplication, addition and
substraction.
Hence,
15 / 5 x 3 + 7 – 15
= 3 x 3 + 7 – 15 (15/3=3, division)
= 9 + 7 – 15 (3x3=9, multiplication)
= 16 – 15 (9+7=16, addition)
= 1 (16-15=1, substraction)
Example 2
Simplify: 55 - 576 ÷ 12 + 11 x 3
Here, 55 - 576 ÷ 12 + 11 x 3
= 55 - 48 + 11 x 3
[by dividing]
= 55 - 48 + 33
[by multiplying]
= 7 + 33
[by substraction]
= 40
[by addition]
